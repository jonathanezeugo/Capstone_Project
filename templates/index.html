US Rig Count

“By the fall of 1918, it was clear that a nation's prosperity, even its very survival, depended on securing a safe, abundant supply of cheap oil.” ― Albert Marrin, Black Gold: The Story of Oil in Our Lives

Americans know all too well the effect the oil industry has on our standard of living. For most of our country's existence, we have largely been net consumers of oil, reliant on the production of our neighbors. Things changed with the fracking revolution of the late 2000's, and oil became more than just an energy source. Supplying several well-paying jobs for the better part of a decade, oil rigs sprouted up all over the country. Unfortunately, market forces both beyond and within our control have derailed the short lived economic boom.

As the domestic rig count is directly related to the health of the oil industry, our group has set out to predict the number of rigs that are currently in operation. We believe that insight of this nature would help the industry and its stakeholders at large have a better grasp on the market swings of a fairly volatile market, and help in financial and production planning of all types.

In order to do this we analyzed supply and demand data from around the world, the price of brent crude, and oil rig counts all over the globe. We poured over the data using using regression algorithms of all kinds before settling on the Random Forest model, which most accurately predicted our testing with the smallest amount of error.

Additionally, we've created visualizations of our dataset that allows for better understanding of the oil markets at large. We've also included the data tables we used for our prediction model and visualizations with interactive features on our Data page.

Our Oil Rig Count Predictor page allows you to predict the domestic rig count using a few highly correlated features.
